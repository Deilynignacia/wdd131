Dickens's Classic Tale

“A Christmas Carol is the universal story of Christmas: a tale of the triumph of compassion over mean-spiritedness, humanity over injustice and poverty. Charles Dickens could write it because he had been there. His father was a chronic debtor, and Dickens himself experienced extreme poverty as a child.”

— Simon Worrall, National Geographic, 24 de diciembre de 2014. Read the full article here.

Every year, as the holiday season arrives, there are stories that come back to life to remind us of the real meaning of the season. One of those magical stories is "A Christmas Carol" by Charles Dickens. And even though it might seem like a classic ghost story, it is much more than that.

"A Christmas Carol" tells the story of Mr. Ebenezer Scrooge, an old man largely known to be greedy and hard-hearted. Scrooge hates Christmas and calls it “Humbug.” This was not always his position, but life experiences have made him depart from all softness and beautiful feelings for the season. It is during a Christmas Eve, while alone and miserly, that he is visited by the ghost of his deceased business partner and best friend, Jacob Marley, followed by three other spirits: the Ghosts of Christmas Past, Present, and Yet to Come. Together, over the course of a single night, they show him the way he has led his life, the people he has hurt, and those who actually cared for him in the past and whom he let go in favor of riches. They help him understand the true meaning of Christmas and the matchless joy of goodness, generosity, and kindness, warming his heart and changing his life.

Dickens's message is as relevant today as it was in 1843. It teaches us that no matter how lost we might feel or how far from the light, there is always a way. There is an opportunity to change our lives for the better, waiting around the corner for us to take it. This classic is a real masterpiece of literature that inspires us to open our hearts and celebrate the joy of giving and reconnecting with our loved ones.
